1. Introduction

In this ebook, we delve into the properties of the function defined by the equation \((1 - \cos(2 \pi x))/x - (\cos(2 \pi k / x) - 1)/z = 0\), where its roots are the divisors of an integer \(k\). We explore this function under \(x > 1\) and \(x \geq 1\), and interpret the reciprocal form as an analysis of \(1/f(x)\). Our goal is to manipulate it for convergence and connect it to the Riemann zeta function, theta function, sinc function, Dirichlet series, and apply a Mellin transform to uncover its number-theoretical significance.

This journey combines rigorous analysis with exploratory hypotheses, offering a comprehensive study and a springboard for further research into this intriguing mathematical object.

2. The Function and Its Roots

Define \(f(x) = \frac{1 - \cos(2 \pi x)}{x} - \frac{\cos(2 \pi k / x) - 1}{z}\). Using identities:

\f(x) = 2 ( \frac{\sin^2(\pi x)}{x} + \frac{\sin^2(\pi k / x)}{z} \)

For \(f(x) = 0\), \(x\) must be an integer divisor of \(k\). For \(x > 1\), roots are divisors \(d > 1\); for \(x \geq 1\), all \(d \geq 1\).

3. Manipulating the Function

Since \(1 / f(x) \sim \frac{1}{c_d (x - d)^2}\) at roots \(d\), we modify it as \(h(x) = \frac{1}{f(x) + \epsilon}\) to ensure convergence of integrals like \(\int_1^\infty h(x) x^{-s} dx\).

4. Connection to the Riemann Zeta Function

The integral \(\int_1^\infty \frac{1}{f(x)} x^{-s} dx\) suggests a sum over divisors, akin to \(\zeta(s)\). Hypothesis: Residue methods link it to \(\sum_{d|k, d>1} d^{-s}\).

5. The Theta Function and Sinc Function

\(\frac{\sin^2(\pi x)}{x}\) relates to \(\text{sinc}(x)\). Theta function connections are hypothesized via periodicity but remain exploratory.

6. Dirichlet Series and Mellin Transform

The Mellin transform of \(1 / f(x)\) relates to \(\sum_{d|k} c_d d^{-s}\), a Dirichlet series over divisors, converging for adjusted forms.

7. Number Theoretical Properties

The roots reflect \(k\)'s divisor structure, potentially encoding \(d(k)\) or related functions through weighted sums.

8. Hypothesis and Further Reading

Hypothesis: Advanced transforms may solidify links to \(\zeta(s)\) and modular forms. Explore Fourier analysis and distribution theory.
