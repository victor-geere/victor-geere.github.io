This analysis relates \( f(x) \) to advanced mathematical frameworks, leveraging its symmetry \( f(x) = f(k/x) \) and oscillatory behavior.

1. Introduction

For \( f(x) = -2 \left[ \sin^2(\pi x) + \sin^2\left( \pi \frac{k}{x} \right) \right] \), if \( x = d \) divides \( k \), then \( \sin(\pi d) = 0 \) and \( \sin\left( \pi \frac{k}{d} \right) = 0 \) (since \( k/d \) is an integer), making \( f(d) = 0 \). The function’s symmetry \( f(x) = f(k/x) \) and periodic components invite connections to number theory and analysis.

2. Riemann Zeta Function

The Riemann zeta function, \( \zeta(s) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^s} \) (\( \Re(s) > 1 \)), extends via analytic continuation with a pole at \( s = 1 \). Its square, \( \zeta(s)^2 = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{d(k)}{k^s} \), sums the divisor function \( d(k) \), but \( f(d) = 0 \) at divisors suggests indirect connections.

The functional equation \( \zeta(s) = 2^s \pi^{s-1} \sin\left( \frac{\pi s}{2} \right) \Gamma(1-s) \zeta(1-s) \) resembles \( f(x) = f(k/x) \). Oscillations in the explicit formula for primes and in \( f(x) \)’s sine terms hint at a link, possibly via the Hurwitz zeta \( \zeta(s, a) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{1}{(n+a)^s} \), where \( \sin(t \log n) \) arises for complex \( s \).

Hypothesis 1: Zeta Symmetry

The symmetry \( f(x) = f(k/x) \) suggests a transform mirroring \( \zeta(s) \)’s functional equation.

Result: The Mellin transform \( \mathcal{M}\{f\}(s) = \int_0^{\infty} x^{s-1} f(x) \, dx \) satisfies \( \mathcal{M}\{f\}(s) = k^{s-1} \mathcal{M}\{f\}(2 - s) \), akin to \( s \to 1-s \), linking \( f(x) \) to zeta-like properties.
