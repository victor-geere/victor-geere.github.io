1. Introduction

The function \( f(x) = -2 \left[ \sin^2(\pi x) + \sin^2\left( \pi \frac{k}{x} \right) \right] \) is zero when \( x \) or \( \frac{k}{x} \) is an integer, specifically at the positive divisors of \( k \). This property suggests deep connections to number theory, which we explore through various advanced mathematical lenses.

2. Riemann Zeta Function

The Riemann zeta function is defined as: \[ \zeta(s) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^s}, \quad \Re(s) > 1 \] with an integral representation and functional equation: \[ \zeta(s) = 2^s \pi^{s-1} \sin\left( \frac{\pi s}{2} \right) \Gamma(1 - s) \zeta(1 - s) \]

The Hurwitz zeta function, \( \zeta(s, a) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{1}{(n + a)^s} \), generalizes \( \zeta(s) \), and its real and imaginary parts involve trigonometric functions, hinting at a link to \( f(x) \).

Connection: The oscillatory terms \( \sin^2(\pi x) \) in \( f(x) \) resemble the sine and cosine oscillations in zeta function expansions. For \( x = d \), a divisor of \( k \), \( f(d) = 0 \), suggesting \( f(x) \) could modulate divisor sums like \( \sum_{d|k} g(d) \). A parametric zeta-like series might be: \[ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} f(n) n^{-s} \] though convergence requires careful handling due to \( f(x) \)’s negative values.

3. Sinc Function and Sine Kernel

The sinc function, \( \text{sinc}(x) = \frac{\sin(\pi x)}{\pi x} \), and the sine kernel, \( \frac{\sin(x - y)}{x - y} \), appear in signal processing and random matrix theory.

Link to \( f(x) \): Using \( \sin^2(\theta) = \frac{1 - \cos(2\theta)}{2} \), \[ f(x) = -\left[ 2 - \cos(2\pi x) - \cos\left(2\pi \frac{k}{x}\right) \right] \] This differs from \( \text{sinc}(x) \), but the oscillatory nature suggests parallels in integral transforms or eigenvalue spacing studies.

4. Mellin Transform

The Mellin transform is: \[ \mathcal{M}\{f\}(s) = \int_{0}^{\infty} x^{s-1} f(x) \, dx \]

For \( f(x) \), compute: \[ \mathcal{M}\{f\}(s) = -2 \int_{0}^{\infty} x^{s-1} \left[ \sin^2(\pi x) + \sin^2\left( \pi \frac{k}{x} \right) \right] \, dx \] Split into two integrals. The first, \( \int_{0}^{\infty} x^{s-1} \sin^2(\pi x) \, dx \), relates to zeta functions via known results, while the second, with substitution \( u = \frac{k}{x} \), leverages the symmetry \( f(x) = f(k/x) \).

Implications: This transform may connect \( f(x) \) to divisor distributions or L-functions, warranting further computation.

5. Euler-Maclaurin Sum Formula

The formula is: \[ \sum_{n=a}^{b} f(n) \approx \int_{a}^{b} f(x) \, dx + \text{correction terms} \]

For \( \sum_{n=1}^{N} f(n) \), the integral \( \int_{1}^{N} f(x) \, dx \) and derivatives of \( f(x) \) (considering its behavior at divisors) could approximate divisor-related sums, linking to \( \zeta(s) \) via asymptotic analysis.

6. p-adic Analysis

In p-adic numbers, consider \( f(x) \) for \( x \) near divisors of \( k \) in the p-adic metric. The p-adic zeta function might interpolate \( f(n) \) at prime powers.

Insight: This could reveal divisor patterns modulo primes, enhancing our understanding of \( k \)’s arithmetic structure.

7. Dirichlet Series

Construct: \[ D(s) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{f(n)}{n^s} \] The sine-log kernel \( \sin(x \log n) \) might approximate \( f(n) \)’s oscillations. Compare to \( \zeta(s)^2 = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{d(n)}{n^s} \), where \( d(n) \) is the divisor count.

Analysis: \( D(s) \) encodes divisor properties, potentially converging with regularization.

8. Convergent Infinite Series

Expand: \[ f(x) = -\left[ 2 - \sum_{m=-\infty}^{\infty} e^{i 2\pi m x} - \sum_{m=-\infty}^{\infty} e^{i 2\pi m \frac{k}{x}} \right] \text{(Fourier form)} \] Damping factors (e.g., \( e^{-\epsilon m^2} \)) ensure convergence, relating to divisor sums via zero locations.

9. Poisson Summation and Theta Function

Poisson summation gives: \[ \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} f(n) = \sum_{m=-\infty}^{\infty} \hat{f}(m) \] The symmetry \( f(x) = f(k/x) \) suggests a Fourier transform relation, possibly linking to theta functions like \( \theta(\tau) = \sum e^{-\pi n^2 \tau} \).

10. Weierstrass Factorization

Since \( f(x) \) has zeros at divisors \( d | k \), attempt: \[ f(x) \propto \prod_{d|k} \left( 1 - \frac{x}{d} \right) \left( 1 - \frac{d}{x} \right) \] Adjust for poles and domain, as \( f(x) \) is not entire.
