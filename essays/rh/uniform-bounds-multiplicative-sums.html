Introduction & Context

This document addresses the second major remaining gap in establishing a rigorous dictionary between cosine phase alignment and the non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function: obtaining sharp, uniform bounds on multiplicative oscillatory sums over primes of the form

\[ \sum_{p \leq x} \cos\bigl(2\pi \, \alpha \, f(\log p)\bigr) \quad \text{or} \quad \sum_{p \leq x} \cos\bigl(2\pi \, \alpha / u_p\bigr), \]

where up = log p / log t and the phase is nonlinear. These sums are crucial for connecting the geometric energy functional

\[ E(x;t,\sigma) = \bigl[1 - \cos(2\pi t/x)\bigr]^2 + \bigl[1 - \cos(2\pi x)\bigr]^2 \]

to the vanishing of \(\Xi(t)\) via the approximate functional equation, explicit formulae, or Fourier representations.

Proposed Expansion: Pathway Toward Uniform Bounds

  1. Short-interval discorrelation + transference principle
    Extend Matomäki–Radziwiłł–Tao machinery (already very strong for polynomials) to the specific reciprocal-type phase 1/up.
  2. Hybrid major/minor arc decomposition
    Use recent log-free rational bounds on major arcs and push minor-arc control via advanced differencing techniques.
  3. Nilsequence approximation + entropy decrement
    Approximate nonlinear phase by nilsequences; leverage recent averaged Chowla-type results.
  4. Conditional progress
    Assume mild improvements in zero-density or subconvexity for twisted L-functions.

Remaining Sub-Gaps to Full Uniform Bounds

  1. Extend short-interval discorrelation to the reciprocal phase 1/u uniformly in t.
  2. Obtain non-trivial saving (better than x1−δ) in the worst-case irrational minor arc for α/up.
  3. Prove an effective version of transference with constants good enough for a positive lower bound on energy independent of height t.
  4. Control contribution of potential low-lying zeros that could create dangerous cancellations.

Conclusion on this Gap

As of January 11, 2026, the decisive uniform nonlinear bound needed to make the cosine-alignment dictionary force destructive interference only on the critical line is still missing.

However, the explosive progress in prime short-interval discorrelation (Matomäki–Radziwiłł–Tao school, 2021–2025) and log-free exponential sum technology makes this the most promising remaining gap that could potentially be resolved in the coming years.
A breakthrough here — even conditional on a modest density improvement — would constitute major progress toward turning the quaternion-cosine geometric intuition into a serious candidate pathway for proving the Riemann Hypothesis.
