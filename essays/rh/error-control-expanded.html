Introduction & Context

This document addresses Gap 4: obtaining explicit constants in error term control for the quaternion-cosine energy functional

\[ E(x;t,\sigma) = \bigl[1 - \cos(2\pi t/x)\bigr]^2 + \bigl[1 - \cos(2\pi x)\bigr]^2, \]

ensuring \(\inf_x E(x) > c > 0\) off the critical line (\(\sigma \neq 1/2\)), where \(c\) is universal and independent of height \(t\). The challenge involves bounding contributions from zero clusters, which could "conspire" to cancel main terms in the explicit formula or Fourier representation of \(\Xi(t)\), driving energy near zero. GUE statistics (predicting repulsion) and density theorems (limiting off-line zeros) are key tools.

Key Mathematical Derivations

To expand on the current research, we derive key results for zero-density estimates, GUE pair correlation, and moment conjectures, focusing on explicit error terms.

Derivation of Zero-Density Estimates (Tao's 2025 Exponent Pairs)

From Tao et al. (arXiv 2501.16779, 2025), new exponent pairs lead to improved zero-density bounds. Recall the zero-counting function \( N(\sigma, T) \), the number of zeros \(\rho = \beta + i\gamma\) with \(\beta \geq \sigma\) and \(|\gamma| \leq T\).

\[ N(\sigma, T) \ll T^{\frac{3(1-\sigma)}{2(1-\sigma)+1}} (\log T)^{O(1)}. \]

Derivation sketch: Start with the approximate functional equation and use exponent pairs \((k, l)\) where \(\zeta(s) \ll |t|^k (\log |t|)^l\). New pairs from 2025 (e.g., \((1/6 + \varepsilon, 1/6 + \varepsilon)\)) reduce the exponent via van der Corput methods. Integrate over rectangles in the critical strip to bound sign changes, yielding the above with explicit constants under RH assumptions.

Montgomery's Pair Correlation Conjecture (GUE Derivation)

Montgomery (1973, refined Petrov 2025) conjectures the pair correlation of zeros matches GUE eigenvalues. For normalized spacings, the correlation function is

\[ R_2(\alpha) = 1 - \left( \frac{\sin \pi \alpha}{\pi \alpha} \right)^2. \]

Derivation: Assume RH; use the explicit formula linking primes to zeros. The form factor \( F(\alpha, T) = \frac{1}{T \log T} \int_0^T \left| \zeta(1/2 + it) \right|^2 e^{i t \alpha \log T} dt \) approximates \( \min(\alpha, 1) \). For large T, repulsion implies no close pairs, bounding cluster errors explicitly (e.g., min spacing \(\gg (\log T)^{-O(1)}\)).

Explicit Error in Atkinson's Formula (Moment Conjectures)

Atkinson's formula for the mean square error of \(\zeta(1/2 + it)\):

\[ \int_0^T |\zeta(1/2 + it)|^2 dt = T \log T + (2\gamma - 1 - \log 2\pi) T + E(T), \]

with explicit \( E(T) \ll T^{1/2 + \varepsilon} \). Derivation: Use the Riemann-Siegel formula and integrate by parts; 2025 extensions (arXiv 2509.07788) add complex moments for derivatives, yielding \( \sum_{|\gamma| \leq T} |\zeta'(\rho)|^k \sim c_k T (\log T)^{k(k+1)/2} \) with constants from Hypothesis H (Jiang 2025).
