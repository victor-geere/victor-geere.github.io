Introduction & Context

This document addresses Gap 3: the rigorous justification of substituting logarithmic phases (arising naturally from the explicit formula, e.g., t log p) with geometric phases (e.g., t/x or reciprocal forms) in the quaternion-cosine framework. This substitution is essential to connect the analytic oscillatory sums over primes to the energy functional

\[ E(x;t,\sigma) = \bigl[1 - \cos(2\pi t/x)\bigr]^2 + \bigl[1 - \cos(2\pi x)\bigr]^2 \]

and thereby force destructive interference (zero of \(\Xi(t)\)) only on the critical line \(\sigma = 1/2\).

The challenge arises because log p is transcendental and lacks direct arithmetic structure, threatening the preservation of the Euler product and functional equation symmetry.

The Berry-Keating Hamiltonian: Core Details & Expansions

The Berry-Keating conjecture (1999) proposes the classical Hamiltonian

\[ H_{cl} = x p \]

(with quantum version \(\hat{H} = \frac{1}{2}(\hat{x}\hat{p} + \hat{p}\hat{x})\)) whose eigenvalues should correspond (asymptotically or exactly) to the imaginary parts of the Riemann zeros after suitable regularization.

Key classical feature: Hyperbolic trajectories in phase space reflect the scaling symmetry of the functional equation \(\xi(s) = \xi(1-s)\).

Recent expansions (2025–2026):

  • Modular-form generalizations encode number-theoretic information, yielding Hamiltonians with energy levels linked to nontrivial zeros (Phys. Rev. A 112, L060201, Dec 2025).
  • Supersymmetric extensions treat zeros as embedded subsequence in richer spectra (Sierra et al., 2025).
  • Local RH proofs via Mellin transforms of oscillator eigenfunctions project onto Berry-Keating subspaces (Srednicki updates & discussions).

Proposed Solutions & Pathways Toward Justification

  1. Mellin-based change-of-variables theorems
    Use Mellin inversion to map explicit-formula sums \(\sum \cos(t \log p)\) to geometric integrals, preserving Euler product structure.
  2. Spectral realization via Berry-Keating operator
    Treat \(\log p\) as approximate eigenvalues; use trace formulas to justify substitution x \sim \log p.
  3. Supersymmetry & torsion control
    Map logarithmic phases to torsional invariants in SUSY partner potentials, aligning only on critical line.
  4. Conditional progress
    Assume mild subconvexity or local RH to bound substitution errors.
  5. Numerical & large-scale verification
    Simulate substitution for high zeros using truncated Berry-Keating + prime kicks (2025 reports up to 52 million zeros).

Remaining Sub-Gaps to Full Justification

  1. Prove global (not just local) equivalence of log phases to geometric via Berry-Keating regularization.
  2. Resolve operator-ordering ambiguities and add precise prime fluctuations for exact matching.
  3. Obtain explicit error constants under Mellin duality and trace formulas.
  4. Integrate with quaternion torsion: show spectral eigenvalues force alignment only at \(\sigma = 1/2\).
  5. Scale numerical evidence beyond t \sim 10^{13}, incorporating GUE repulsion for clusters.
  6. Remove conditional assumptions (subconvexity, local RH variants) for unconditional justification.

Conclusion on this Gap

As of January 11, 2026, the phase substitution remains partially justified through Mellin transforms and the Berry-Keating spectral framework — especially via 2025 modular generalizations and local RH proofs. The explosive progress in supersymmetric embeddings and large-scale numerical verifications makes this one of the most actively advancing gaps.

A decisive breakthrough in global spectral realization of the Berry-Keating paradigm (even conditional on modest subconvexity) would significantly elevate the quaternion-cosine geometric intuition toward a serious candidate pathway for proving the Riemann Hypothesis.
