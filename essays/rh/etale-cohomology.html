Introduction

The Weil conjectures (proven by Deligne in 1974) established an analogue of the Riemann Hypothesis for zeta functions of varieties over finite fields using étale cohomology with ℓ-adic coefficients. This remains the deepest geometric proof of any Riemann-Hypothesis-type statement.

The speculative dyadic sheaf cohomology construction attempts to mimic this success in the arithmetic setting of the adele ring \(\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q}\) and the Riemann zeta function. This document compares the two theories side-by-side, highlighting analogies, differences, and why the dyadic approach — while extremely distant — might offer a new conceptual path.

1. Quick Overview of ÉTale Cohomology

Étale cohomology (Grothendieck, Artin, Verdier, Deligne, 1960s–1970s) is the right cohomology theory for algebraic varieties in characteristic p that behaves like singular cohomology in characteristic 0 but is compatible with algebraic geometry.

Key features:
  • Site: étale site (étale morphisms instead of open immersions)
  • Coefficients: ℓ-adic sheaves (\(\mathbb{Z}_\ell\), \(\mathbb{Q}_\ell\), \(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_\ell\)) for ℓ ≠ p
  • Frobenius endomorphism acts on cohomology
  • Lefschetz trace formula: \(\zeta(X,T) = \exp\left( \sum_{i=0}^{2\dim} (-1)^i \operatorname{Tr}(\operatorname{Frob}_q^i \mid H^i_{\text{ét}}(X_{\overline{k}},\mathbb{Q}_\ell)\right) \log T \)
  • Weil conjectures: eigenvalues of Frobenius on \(H^i\) have absolute value \(q^{i/2}\)
The RH analogue follows from the fixed-point interpretation and purity of weights.

2. Recap of the Proposed Dyadic Sheaf Cohomology

The dyadic construction (pure speculation, 2026) attempts to build an analogous theory over the adele ring using a dyadic filtration instead of étale topology.

Core elements:
  • Base: multiplicative group of ideles \(\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q}^\times\) (or quotient by \(\mathbb{Q}^\times\))
  • Sheaves: \(\mathcal{F}_0\) (dyadic locally constant functions), \(\mathcal{F}_1 = \mathcal{F}_0 \otimes \chi_{\mathrm{dyadic}}\)
  • Complex: \( 0 \to \mathcal{F}_0 \xrightarrow{d} \mathcal{F}_1 \to 0 \)
  • Differential: conjectural dyadic difference operator
  • Anti-involution \(\tau\) encoding functional equation symmetry, with \(\tau^2 = \mathrm{id}\) iff \(\Re(s) = 1/2\)
  • Goal: \( H^1 = 0 \) off the critical line, \(\dim H^1 = \) multiplicity on the line

4. Deep Analogies

  • Both replace a difficult analytic problem (RH / Weil conjectures) with algebraic geometry + cohomology
  • Both rely on a symmetry/endomorphism whose eigenvalues/weights encode the location of zeros
  • Both use a trace formula or Euler characteristic to relate cohomology to zeta functions
  • Both hope for a Lefschetz-type fixed-point theorem (Frobenius vs. reflection symmetry)
  • Étale succeeded in char p because of the existence of Frobenius; the dyadic idea hopes that the global reflection symmetry \(s \mapsto 1-s\) plays an analogous structural role

5. Fundamental Differences & Obstacles

  • No natural “Frobenius” in characteristic zero/global arithmetic
  • Étale works over finite fields (compact / discrete); ideles are infinite-dimensional and non-compact
  • ℓ-adic continuity vs. dyadic discreteness (binary vs. p-adic)
  • Étale has purity of weights theorem; no analogue yet for the dyadic setting
  • Defining a natural differential \(d\) and proving convergence/regularization is a massive open problem
  • Proving the anti-involution squares to identity only on the critical line seems extremely difficult

6. Potential Advantages of the Dyadic Approach

  • Uses the dyadic (binary) structure that appears in many effective number-theoretic calculations
  • Directly incorporates the functional equation as an anti-involution of the complex (not just of the function)
  • Possibly allows a maximum principle on adelic height to force acyclicity off-line
  • Works uniformly at every place (including archimedean), unlike p-adic theories

If the dyadic filtration can be made to behave like a “global étale site” for arithmetic purposes, it might provide the missing geometric structure for RH.

Conclusion & Outlook

Étale cohomology gave the deepest known proof of an RH analogue. The dyadic sheaf cohomology is a highly speculative attempt to find an analogous geometric theory over the adeles. While the analogies are intriguing, the differences are profound and the obstacles enormous. This direction remains a thought experiment — but one that deliberately tries to learn from the single greatest success in RH-analogue proofs.
