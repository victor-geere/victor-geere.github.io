Introduction

This ebook distills a long conversation into a coherent, actionable framework that combines the Kalman filter (for clean state estimation) with Shannon’s Ghost (the volatility-harvesting rebalancing strategy). The result is a robust, asymptotically profitable system designed for high-volatility assets like Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, and Polkadot.

The Efficient Frontier & Rebalancing

Harry Markowitz showed that the optimal risk-return trade-off lies on the efficient frontier. Rebalancing keeps a portfolio on that curve by correcting drift.

The Efficient Frontier with a drifting portfolio (blue dot) needing rebalancing

Kalman Filter – Your Noise-Cancelling Brain

The Kalman filter recursively estimates the true price (and optionally velocity/volatility) from noisy market data.

\[ \hat{x}_k = F \hat{x}_{k-1} + K_k (z_k - H \hat{x}_{k-1}) \]

Prediction + correction step. \(K_k\) is the Kalman gain that optimally blends prior belief and new measurement.

Shannon’s Ghost – Pure Volatility Harvesting

Claude Shannon’s thought experiment: rebalance constantly between a volatile asset and cash. Even with zero drift, geometric returns are positive because you repeatedly “buy low, sell high”.

Ghost rebalancing transfers wealth from volatile side to stable side

The Hybrid System: Kalman + Ghost + Fractional Kelly

Split your float into four equal parts:

  • 25% Operational BTC – used by the Ghost
  • 25% Operational USD – cash leg of Ghost
  • 25% Reserve BTC – feeds the Kalman filter
  • 25% Reserve USD – funding source

Kalman watches the reserve BTC price → produces clean drift (μ) and volatility (σ) estimates → decides when to top-up the operational float or lock profits.

Drift vs Volatility Math

Breakeven volatility needed to overcome negative drift:

\[ \sigma > \sqrt{2 |\mu| / \kappa} \]

μ = hourly drift, κ = mean-reversion speed (typically ~0.02 for crypto)

Example (DOT-USD past year): μ ≈ –0.01 %/hour, σ ≈ 85 % annualized → easily covers downside.

Fractional Kelly Sizing

\[ f^* = \frac{\mu}{\sigma^2} \quad \text{(full Kelly)} \] \[ f_{\text{used}} = c \cdot f^* \quad c \in [0.25, 0.75] \]

Use low c (0.25) in mean-reverting regimes, higher c (0.5–0.75) when Kalman detects strong positive drift.
