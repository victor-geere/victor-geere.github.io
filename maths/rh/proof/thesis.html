Chapter 2: Bruhat–Tits Trees and Transfer Operators

Geometry of the Bruhat–Tits Tree \(T_p\)

The Bruhat–Tits tree \(T_p\) associated to \(\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Q}_p)\) is the unique (up to isomorphism) \((p+1)\)-regular tree. Its vertices correspond to homothety classes of \(\mathbb{Z}_p\)-lattices in \(\mathbb{Q}_p^2\). Let \(\vec{\mathcal{E}}\) denote the set of directed edges. The forward shift map \(\tau: \vec{\mathcal{E}} \to \vec{\mathcal{E}}\) sends each directed edge to the unique outgoing edge at its target vertex that is not the reverse edge. Each edge has exactly \(p\) pre-images under \(\tau\).

Geometric Transfer Operator

Definition (Geometric transfer operator). The operator \(L_{p,s}\) on \(\ell^2(\vec{\mathcal{E}})\) is defined by \[ (L_{p,s} f)(e) = p^{-s} \sum_{\substack{e' \in \vec{\mathcal{E}} \\ \tau(e') = e}} f(e'). \]

By standard analysis using finite-depth truncations and the Ihara zeta function for regular trees (Bass, Lubotzky; Deitmar–Kang), the regularised Fredholm determinant satisfies

\[ \det_*(1 - L_{p,s}) = 1 - p^{-s}. \]
