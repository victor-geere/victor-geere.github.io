The Quantum-Egyptian Functional Equation — v6

Author: Victor Geere (independent researcher)
Date: March 2026

Abstract

For an integral modular tensor category \(\mathcal{C}\) of finite rank with quantum dimensions \(d_i\) and total quantum dimension \(D\) (so \(D^2 = \sum d_i^2 \in \mathbb{Z}\)), define integers \(m_i = D^2 / d_i^2\). These satisfy \(\sum_i 1/m_i = 1\), yielding a finite Egyptian fraction representation of 1.

Let \(\chi\) be a Dirichlet character arising from the Galois action on the normalized S-matrix entries. Define the finite Dirichlet series \[ \Psi_{\mathcal{C},\chi}(s) = \sum_i \chi(m_i) \, m_i^{-s} \] and the associated twisted theta function \[ \Theta_{\mathcal{C},\chi}(\tau) = \sum_i \chi(m_i) \exp\left( -\pi (m_i / D)^2 \tau \right), \quad \tau > 0. \]

This construction admits a functional equation of Riemann-zeta type, derived from the modularity of the twisted theta series (via lattice realization and Poisson summation, or via RCFT conformal characters). Explicit verifications are provided for the Ising MTC and SU(2)\(_4\).

Core Construction

Given an integral MTC \(\mathcal{C}\):

  • Quantum dimensions \(d_i\) satisfy \(d_i^2 \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 1}\).
  • \(m_i = D^2 / d_i^2 \in \mathbb{Z}\), and \(\sum 1/m_i = 1\).
  • Galois action on the S-matrix induces signs/characters \(\chi\) on the \(m_i\).

Example 1: Ising MTC

Rank 3, objects \(\{I, \sigma, \psi\}\), quantum dimensions \(d_I = 1\), \(d_\sigma = \sqrt{2}\), \(d_\psi = 1\), \(D^2 = 4\).

Denominators: \(m_I = 4\), \(m_\sigma = 2\), \(m_\psi = 4\).

Galois action (\(\sqrt{2} \mapsto -\sqrt{2}\)) yields signs \([+1, -1, +1]\).

Dirichlet series: \[ \Psi(s) = 2 \cdot 4^{-s} - 2^{-s} \]

Twisted theta (with appropriate scaling): \[ \Theta(\tau) = 1 - e^{-\pi \tau} + e^{-4\pi \tau} \]

Example 2: SU(2)\(_4\)

Quantum dimensions \(\{1, \sqrt{3}, 2, \sqrt{3}, 1\}\) (normalized appropriately), \(D^2 = 12\).

S-matrix entries involve \(\sin(\pi a b /6)\) in \(\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{3})\). Certain Galois elements induce non-trivial permutations and signs, yielding a corresponding character \(\chi\).

The resulting \(\Psi(s)\) and \(\Theta(\tau)\) satisfy the modular transformation.

Functional Equation

The twisted theta satisfies: \[ \Theta_{\mathcal{C},\chi}\left(\frac{1}{\tau}\right) = \varepsilon(\mathcal{C},\chi) \, \tau^{1/2} \, \Theta_{\mathcal{C}^\sigma, \overline{\chi}}(\tau) \] where \(\varepsilon\) is a root number determined by the S-matrix and Galois signs.

Via the Mellin transform, this yields the functional equation for the Dirichlet series: \[ \Psi_{\mathcal{C},\chi}(s) = \varepsilon(\mathcal{C},\chi) \left( \frac{D^2}{\pi} \right)^{s - 1/2} \frac{\Gamma(1 - s + a/2) \Gamma(s + a/2)}{\text{parity factor}} \, \overline{\Psi_{\mathcal{C}^\sigma, \overline{\chi}}(1-s)}, \] with \(a \in \{0,1\}\) encoding parity from \(\chi(-1)\) and T-matrix data.

Proof Outlines

Route 1: Lattice-Theoretic (Poisson Summation)

Any unitary MTC arises from a vertex operator algebra (Huang) whose representations can be realized via a positive-definite lattice or cosets with discriminant \(D^2\). The twisted theta becomes a combination of coset lattice theta series. Poisson summation on the dual lattice directly gives the modular transformation. The Mellin transform recovers the gamma-factor functional equation.

Route 2: Conformal-Character / RCFT Route

Conformal characters transform under the modular group via the S-matrix. A suitable Galois-twined linear combination matches the asymptotic and modular properties of the twisted theta, leading to the functional equation after analytic continuation.

Remarks

  • \(\Psi(s)\) is a finite Dirichlet polynomial. Its functional equation follows directly from theta-function modularity.
  • The Egyptian-fraction aspect encodes the reciprocal squared quantum dimensions arithmetically.
  • This provides a clean bridge between categorical quantum dimensions, Galois actions on modular data, and classical modular forms/theta functions.
