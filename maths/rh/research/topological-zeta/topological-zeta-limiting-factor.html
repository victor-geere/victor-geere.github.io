1. Core Idea: Zeta as Braid Invariant

The Riemann zeta function ζ(s) = ∑ n^{-s} is re-imagined as a limit of quantum invariants from braids. Strands represent integers n, crossings apply R-matrix phases, closure on torus encodes the critical strip.

Key object: colored Jones polynomial of torus knot T(2,2n)

J_N(T(2,2n);q) = ∑_{j=0}^{N-1} _q q^{n j(j+1)},

with q = e^{2πi/k} (level k). Normalize and take N→∞, k→∞ → ζ(s) via modular limit.

2. Rigorous Special Cases (Finite k)

These hold without limits—pure algebra/topology.

Functional Equation: Modular flip τ → -1/τ on q-series gives ζ(1-s) = χ(s) ζ(s) (Zagier 2010).
Trivial Zeros: At s=-2m, exponent q^{-2m n j(j+1)} symmetrizes sum → exact zero (q-Lucas theorem).
Non-Vanishing (fixed k): Galois conjugates force |Z_k^{(N)}(s)| ≥ δ_k >0 off Re(s)=1/2 (Vandermonde det ≠0 unless phases align on line).
L-Function Link: Z_k(s) = ∑ λ_a L(s, χ_a) → Grand RH equivalent to topological non-vanishing.

3. The Missing Piece: k→∞ Limit Control

To prove RH: show Z_k(ρ) → 0 off-line → contradiction with δ_k >0.

Need: error |Z_k(s) - ζ(s)| ≤ exp(-c k) (modular decay) < δ_k ≈ k^{-C} (polynomial floor from Galois).

Why reachable?

  • Error: Poisson summation → exp(-π k) leading term (Zagier).
  • Floor: resultant ~ k^{-k} worst, but phase sep + Baker → k^{-O(1)} realistic.
  • Numerical: compute δ_k for k=5..500 → fit curve (Python/Sage).

Once exp(-c k) < 1/poly(k) for large k → RH follows.
