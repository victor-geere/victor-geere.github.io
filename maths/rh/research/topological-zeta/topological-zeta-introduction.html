Chapter 1: From Analytic Series to Braided Topology

The Riemann zeta function ζ(s) = ∑ n^{-s} (Re(s)>1) extends analytically to ℂ. In our view, it is not a sum—but a braided object in Chern-Simons TQFT.

  • Strands: integers n=1,2,… weighted n^{-s}
  • Crossings: R-matrix phases encode alternating signs (-1)^{n-1} (eta twist)
  • Closure: torus knot representing the critical strip

The Reshetikhin-Turaev invariant RT_k(B_s) approximates ζ(s) for large k.

Chapter 2: The Critical Line as Toroidal Symmetry

The critical line Re(s)=1/2 is the toroidal closure axis: braid flip s → 1-s mirrors modular duality τ → -1/τ. Sin(π s / 2) arises from R-matrix rotations.

Functional equation: ζ(1-s) = 2^{1-s} π^{s-1} sin(π s / 2) Γ(s) ζ(s) → braid orientation flip with phase balance.

Chapter 3: Zeros as Topological Defects

Theorem (Speculative Topological Zeta Rigidity)
All non-trivial zeros of ζ(s) lie on Re(s)=1/2 ⇔ every RT_k(B_ρ)=0 only when B_ρ closes symmetrically under reflection, with no off-axis defects (unlinks, phase imbalances).

Chapter 4: Lemma – Asymmetry Implies Non-Zero Trace

Lemma
If ρ = σ + it with σ ≠ 1/2, then reflection s → 1-s mismatches strand heights t, yielding net phase δ ≠ 0 mod 2π. Reidemeister invariance forbids symmetrization without unlink → RT_k(B_ρ) ≠ 0.

Proof: Phase mismatch from sin(π σ / 2) ≠ ±1; topological obstruction prevents balance.

Chapter 5: Contradiction & Conclusion

Assume off-line zero ρ: ζ(ρ)=0 ⇒ RT_k(B_ρ)=0. But lemma says trace ≠0 → contradiction. Hence no off-line zeros. RH holds.

Q.E.D. (in this speculative framework)

Chapter 6: Caveats & Frontier Status

This is not a formal proof. RT invariants compute knot polynomials (Jones, HOMFLY), not zeta directly. Analytic continuation isn't topological. The "defect" analogy is poetic—no literature bridges them rigorously. Still, it offers a fresh lens: zeta as braid-invariant, zeros as unbreakable symmetry.
