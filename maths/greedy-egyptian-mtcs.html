Constructing the Greedy-Egyptian Modular Tensor Categories

Detailed Research Task • Phase 5 of the Unified Theory

1. Objective

Rigorously define a new class of modular tensor categories (\(\text{MTC}_{\text{GE}}\)) whose objects and morphisms are derived directly from the greedy harmonic tree and Egyptian fraction decompositions. Prove that these categories are modular and that their associated topological zeta functions encode classical zeta functions.

2. Proposed Construction

Step 1: Objects

Let \(\mathcal{T}\) be the greedy harmonic tree. Define the set of objects \(\text{Ob}(\text{MTC}_{\text{GE}})\) as the set of all finite cylinder intervals (including the root \([0, \pi]\)) together with formal direct sums:

\[X = \bigoplus_{i=1}^k I_{\varepsilon_i}, \quad \text{where } I_{\varepsilon_i} \text{ are cylinder intervals}\]

Step 2: Morphisms (Hom-spaces)

For two objects \(X = \bigoplus I_{\varepsilon}\) and \(Y = \bigoplus I_{\delta}\), define:

\[\text{Hom}(X, Y) = \bigoplus_{\varepsilon, \delta} \mathbb{C} \cdot \text{span}\{ \text{paths from } I_\varepsilon \text{ to } I_\delta \text{ in } \mathcal{T} \}\]

Composition is given by concatenation of paths, with relations coming from the greedy splitting rule.

Step 3: Tensor Product

Define the tensor product using the binary tree structure:

\[I_\varepsilon \otimes I_\delta = I_{\varepsilon \cdot \delta} \quad \text{(concatenation of prefixes)}\]

Extend by linearity to direct sums. The unit object is the root interval \([0, \pi]\).

Step 4: Braiding

Define the braiding isomorphism using the Egyptian fraction decomposition of the angle between intervals:

\[c_{X,Y} : X \otimes Y \to Y \otimes X\]

The braiding is constructed so that it satisfies the hexagon axioms by the recursive length rules of the greedy tree.

Step 5: Rigidity and Pivotal Structure

Define dual objects using the complementary intervals in the tree:

\[X^* = \bigoplus \text{complementary cylinders}\]

The pivotal structure is induced by the natural orientation of the intervals.

Step 6: Modularity

To prove modularity, show that the S-matrix (defined via the trace of the double braiding) is invertible. This is expected to follow from the density of the greedy harmonic expansion and the resulting non-degeneracy of the pairing.

\[S_{ij} = \text{tr}(c_{X_i, X_j} \circ c_{X_j, X_i})\]

3. Key Theorems to Prove

  1. \(\text{MTC}_{\text{GE}}\) is a ribbon category.
  2. The S-matrix is unitary (hence invertible) for finite truncations and converges in the infinite limit.
  3. The topological zeta function of \(\text{MTC}_{\text{GE}}\) equals \(\zeta(s)\) (up to entire factors).
  4. The category is modular when restricted to objects generated by intervals of length \(\le \pi/2\).

4. Open Technical Challenges

  • Proving that the braiding is well-defined and independent of the choice of Egyptian decomposition.
  • Establishing the non-degeneracy of the S-matrix without assuming the Riemann Hypothesis.
  • Handling infinite direct sums rigorously (use completion in the category of Hilbert spaces or von Neumann algebras).
