Proving the Harmonic-Geometric Unified Theory

Overall Goal

Establish the Harmonic-Geometric Unified Theory as a rigorous framework that derives the Riemann zeta function and its generalizations from the greedy harmonic expansion of angles, and prove the Spectral Riemann Hypothesis together with its generalizations.

Operator-Theoretic Completion (9–12 months)

Objective: Rigorously establish the properties of the transfer operator \(\mathcal{L}_s\) and complete the proof of the Fredholm determinant identity.

  • Prove that \(\mathcal{L}_s\) is trace-class for \(\operatorname{Re} s > 1\) with explicit trace-norm bounds.
  • Complete the analytic continuation of \(\mathcal{L}_s\) as a compact operator-valued function for \(\operatorname{Re} s > 1/2\).
  • Fill all gaps in the periodic-orbit trace computation that converts \(\zeta(2s+1)/\zeta(2s)\) into \(\zeta(s)/\zeta(2s)\).
  • Prove that the entire factor \(e^{P(s)}\) is zero-free in the critical strip (currently the weakest link).
  • Publish a paper titled “The Fredholm Determinant Identity for the Greedy Harmonic Transfer Operator”.

3

Spectral Equivalence (12–18 months)

Objective: Prove the Spectral Riemann Hypothesis.

  • Establish the precise correspondence between zeros of \(\zeta(s)\) and eigenvalues of \(\mathcal{L}_{1/2+it}\).
  • Prove that \(\det_{(2)}(I - \mathcal{L}_{1/2+it}) = 0\) if and only if \(\zeta(\frac12 + it) = 0\).
  • Develop a rigorous version of the argument principle for the regularised determinant on the critical line.
  • Investigate the distribution of eigenvalues of \(\mathcal{L}_{1/2+it}\) numerically and theoretically.

4

Generalisation to Dirichlet L-Functions (12–15 months)

Objective: Extend the framework to all primitive Dirichlet characters and prove the Monotonicity Conjecture.

  • Construct the twisted transfer operator \(\mathcal{L}_{s,\chi}\) rigorously.
  • Prove the corresponding Fredholm determinant identity for Harmonic Sine L-Functions.
  • Prove the key monotonicity inequality: \[\operatorname{Im} \operatorname{tr}\Bigl[(I - \mathcal{L}_{1/2+it,\chi})^{-1} \frac{d}{dt} \mathcal{L}_{1/2+it,\chi}\Bigr] \ge 0\]
  • Derive the Generalised Riemann Hypothesis from the monotonicity conjecture.
