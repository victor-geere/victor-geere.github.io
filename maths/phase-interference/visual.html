1. Log-Time Remainder Map (Sawtooth Flow)

The classical Berry–Keating flow \(x(\tau)=\{x_0 e^\tau\}\) (where \(\tau=\log(n+2)\)) is the hyperbolic sawtooth map generated by \(H_{\rm cl}=xp\). The greedy remainder process \(x_{n+1}=(1+1/(n+2))(x_n-\mathbf{1}_{x_n\geq1})\) exactly discretises this flow. Coloured trajectories illustrate different initial conditions; dots mark integer crossings.

2. First-Hit Cylinders & Harmonic Lengths

The Markov partition \(\{K_n=[1/(n+2),1/(n+1))\}\) with lengths \(\ell_n=1/((n+1)(n+2))\) exactly tiles \([0,1)\). These cylinders form the support of the greedy Haar-wavelet basis and induce the transmission domain of the quantised Berry–Keating operator.

3. Explicit Quadratic Local Factors \(Q_n(s)\)

Each cylinder \(K_n\) contributes a quadratic factor arising from the 2×2 Galerkin block (scaling + wavelet) of \(H=-i(x\partial_x + 1/2)\). These are the building blocks of the continued-product representation of \(\det(I-\mathcal{L}_s)\).

4. Hilbert–Pólya Spectrum from Greedy Wavelets

First 20 imaginary parts \(t_k\) of the non-trivial zeros of \(\zeta(s)\), extracted from the finite-depth wavelet compression of \(H\) at depth \(D=20\). The vertical lines are the eigenvalues of the sparse Hermitian matrix \(H^{(20)}\).

5. Explicit Hadamard Product for \(\xi(s)\) from the Greedy Tree

The regularised product over all \(Q_n(s)\) realises the genus-1 Hadamard factorisation of \(\xi(s)\). The plot shows \(\log|\prod_n Q_n(1/2+it)|\) along the critical line; dips locate Riemann zeros (red dashes).
