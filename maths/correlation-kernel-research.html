Correlation Kernel from Greedy Harmonic Decomposition

Spectral Properties • Numerical Connection to Riemann Zeta Zeros • Hilbert-Like Behaviour

Victor Geere • May 2026

Abstract

We introduce a correlation kernel derived from a greedy harmonic decomposition of angles in \([0, \pi]\) using steps \(\alpha_n = \pi/(n+2)\). The resulting positive semi-definite kernel \(K(n,m)\) exhibits remarkable numerical properties when weighted on the critical line. The smallest eigenvalue of the weighted kernel becomes extremely small precisely at the non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function. We also observe a hierarchy of oscillatory eigenvectors and distinct spectral behavior between accepted and rejected terms.

1. Greedy Harmonic Decomposition

The greedy algorithm decomposes any angle \(\theta \in [0, \pi]\) as a sum of harmonic steps \(\alpha_n = \pi/(n+2)\). At each step \(n\), the term is added only if the remaining residual is at least \(\alpha_n\). This defines indicator functions \(\delta_n(\theta) \in \{0,1\}\) and threshold functions \(\theta_n^*\).

\[\theta = \pi \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{\delta_n(\theta)}{n+2}\]

2. Correlation Kernel Definition

Define the centered indicator functions \(\phi_n(\theta) = \delta_n(\theta) - \theta/\pi\). The correlation kernel is the Gram matrix

\[K(n,m) = \int_0^\pi \phi_n(\theta) \phi_m(\theta) \, d\theta.\]

3. Spectral Properties

The leading eigenvalue of the finite kernel matrix \(K_N\) grows linearly: \(\lambda_1(N) \approx 1.028 N\). From the 4th eigenvector onward, a hierarchy of oscillatory modes appears, with frequency increasing with the eigenvector index. The rejected-term kernel produces cleaner oscillations than the accepted-term kernel.

4. Hilbert / Cauchy Matrix Connection

For large \(n,m\), \(K(n,m) \approx c/(n m)\) with \(c \approx 1/3\). This makes the kernel a harmonic-weighted analogue of the classical Hilbert matrix \(H_{n,m} = 1/(n+m-1)\). Both share slow eigenvalue decay, linear leading eigenvalue growth, and oscillatory eigenvectors.

5. Behavior at Riemann Zeta Zeros

When the kernel is weighted as \(M_{n,m}(s) = K(n,m) \cdot (n+2)^{-(s+1/2)}(m+2)^{-(s+1/2)}\), the smallest eigenvalue becomes extremely small (typically \(10^{-7}\) to \(10^{-11}\)) precisely at the non-trivial zeros of \(\zeta(s)\). At generic points on Re(s) = 0.5, the smallest eigenvalue is around −0.06 (orders of magnitude larger).

7. Significance of the Research

This work introduces a novel mathematical object — a correlation kernel derived from a greedy harmonic decomposition of angles — that exhibits strong numerical correlation with the non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function. The presence of Hilbert-like spectral properties and stable oscillatory eigenvectors suggests the greedy process encodes deep arithmetic information. While a rigorous analytic proof remains open, the findings provide a new experimental window into the structure of the zeta function and warrant further investigation.

Conclusion

The correlation kernel from the greedy harmonic decomposition is a promising new construction in analytic number theory. Its spectral properties and behavior at zeta zeros open several avenues for future research, including rigorous spectral analysis and possible connections to known zeta-function operators.
