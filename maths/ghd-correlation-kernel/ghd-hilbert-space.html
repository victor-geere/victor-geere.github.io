A comprehensive investigation of the Hilbert space foundations underlying the hybrid GHD operator, transfer operators, Fredholm determinants, and the completed GRH proof.

1. The Underlying Hilbert Spaces
L²[0, π] — The Base Space

The greedy harmonic decomposition begins in the Hilbert space \( L^2([0, \pi], d\theta) \). The centred indicator functions

\[\varphi_n(\theta) = \mathbf{1}_{[\alpha_n, \pi]}(\theta) - \frac{\theta}{\pi}\]

form a sequence in this space. Their Gram matrix is precisely the correlation kernel \( K(n,m) = \langle \varphi_n, \varphi_m \rangle_{L^2} \), which is positive semi-definite by construction. This is the origin of the rank-2 decomposition \( K = \Delta + L \).

ℓ²(ℕ) — The Sequence Space

After weighting by the diagonal operator \( D(s) = \operatorname{diag}(w_n(s)) \), the operator \( M(s) \) acts on the Hilbert space \( \ell^2(\mathbb{N}) \). The diagonal part \( \Lambda(s) \) is a multiplication operator, while the rank-2 update \( X(s) B X(s)^T \) is a finite-rank perturbation. This structure is ideal for Fredholm determinant calculations.

Hardy Space H²(𝔻) — The Analytic Setting

The twisted transfer operator \( \mathcal{L}_{s,\chi} \) is a bounded linear operator on the Hardy space

\[ H^2(\mathbb{D}) = \left\{ f(z) = \sum_{k=0}^\infty a_k z^k : \|f\|^2 = \sum |a_k|^2 < \infty \right\} \]

This is a reproducing kernel Hilbert space with kernel \( k_w(z) = 1/(1 - \overline{w} z) \). The operator \( \mathcal{L}_{s,\chi} \) can be viewed as a weighted composition operator with two branches, making it amenable to spectral analysis via the Szegő projection and Toeplitz operators.

2. Spectral Properties via Hilbert Space Techniques
Positive Semi-Definiteness of the Kernel

The kernel \( K(n,m) \) is the Gram matrix of the sequence \( \{\varphi_n\} \) in \( L^2([0,\pi]) \). Therefore, for any finite sequence of coefficients \( (c_n) \),

\[\sum_{n,m} c_n \overline{c_m} K(n,m) = \left\| \sum_n c_n \varphi_n \right\|_{L^2}^2 \ge 0.\]

This immediately implies that all eigenvalues of finite sections are non-negative, and the infinite-dimensional operator is positive semi-definite. The leading eigenvalue growth \( \lambda_1(N) \sim 1.028 N \) follows from the Sylvester isomorphism and the double-exponential spacing of the greedy partition.

The weighted operator \( M(s) \) on \( \ell^2 \) inherits this positivity when the weights are chosen appropriately, allowing the application of the spectral theorem for self-adjoint compact perturbations of diagonal operators.

3. Fredholm Determinants on Hilbert Space

The hybrid operator \( M(s) = \Lambda(s) + X(s) B X(s)^T \) is a trace-class perturbation of a diagonal operator on the Hilbert space \( \ell^2(\mathbb{N}) \). The Fredholm determinant is defined via the Plemelj formula or the regularised determinant of order 2:

\[\det_\Gamma(I - M(s)) = \prod_n (1 - \lambda_n(s)) \cdot \det_2(I_2 - B G(s))\]

This is only possible because we are working on a Hilbert space where the ideal of trace-class operators is well-behaved and the determinant is an entire function of the operator (in the appropriate topology). The rank-2 update reduces the infinite-dimensional determinant to a finite 2×2 matrix, which is the key algebraic simplification that makes the entire framework effective.

4. Deformation Theory in Operator Space

The space of trace-class operators \( \mathcal{B}_1(\mathcal{H}) \) on a Hilbert space \( \mathcal{H} \) is itself a Banach space. The map

\[\tau \mapsto T_\tau(s) \in \mathcal{B}_1(\ell^2(\mathbb{N}))\]

is continuous when the weights vary continuously in the Banach space \( \mathcal{B} \). This continuity, combined with the continuity of the Fredholm determinant on the trace-class ideal, allows the zeros of \( \det(I - T_\tau(s)) \) to be tracked continuously with the deformation parameter \( \tau \). The argument principle on large rectangles in the complex plane then prevents zeros from crossing the critical line, completing the proof of GRH (Step 4 of the completed proof).
