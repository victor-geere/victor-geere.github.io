Context & Fredholm-Determinant Identity

The error term \(P_\chi(s)\) appears in the exact identity

\[ \Delta_\chi(s) := \det_{(2)}(I - \mathcal{L}_{s,\chi}) = \frac{L(s,\chi)}{L(2s,\chi^2)} \exp\bigl(P_\chi(s)\bigr), \]

where \(\mathcal{L}_{s,\chi}\) is the twisted transfer operator whose kernel is built from the correlation kernel \(K(n,m)\) (see correlation-kernel.html and harmonic-sine-l-kernel.html).

The function \(P_\chi(s)\) is defined via the regularised Fredholm expansion

\[ P_\chi(s) = \sum_{k=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{k+1}}{k} \operatorname{Tr}_{\mathrm{reg}}\bigl(\mathcal{L}_{s,\chi}^k\bigr). \]

Each trace expands into an orbit-sum representation (derived from the greedy harmonic sieve):

\[ \operatorname{Tr}\bigl(\mathcal{L}_{s,\chi}^k\bigr) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{\chi(n+2)^k}{(n+2)^{ks}} \cdot \mu_k\bigl(\delta_n(\theta)\bigr), \]

where \(\mu_k(\delta_n(\theta))\) is the multiplicity of the \(k\)-fold greedy orbit (explicitly computable from the Sylvester-sequence structure – see hst-sylvester-sequence.html).

Expanded Statement of the Uniform Bound

Theorem 2.1 (Uniform Bound on \(|P_\chi(s)|\) – Expanded Version)
For every primitive Dirichlet character \(\chi\) of conductor \(q\) and every \(s\) with \(\operatorname{Re}(s) \ge 1/2\), \[ |P_\chi(s)| \le C \cdot q^{1/2} \log(2 + |s|), \] where \(C > 0\) is an absolute constant independent of \(\chi\) and \(s\).

This bound is sufficient to control the derivative term in the monotonicity argument (Theorem 3.1 of monotonicity.html).

Expanded Proof (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Bound on orbit multiplicities \(\mu_k\)

From the Sylvester isomorphism (see hst-sylvester-sequence.html, Theorem 4.1), the greedy harmonic sieve produces multiplicities satisfying

\[ \mu_k(\delta_n(\theta)) \le E^k, \]

where \(E \approx 1.264084735305301\) is the classical Sylvester constant. This bound follows from the double-exponential growth of the Sylvester sequence \(s_{n+1} = s_n^2 - s_n + 1\).

Step 2: Absolute-value estimate on individual trace terms

For \(\operatorname{Re}(s) \ge 1/2\)

\[ \left| \frac{\chi(n+2)^k}{(n+2)^{ks}} \right| \le (n+2)^{-k \operatorname{Re}(s)} \le (n+2)^{-k/2}. \]

(Here we used \(|\chi(m)| \le 1\) for any Dirichlet character.)

Step 3: Summation over \(n\) using the Riemann zeta function

Interchanging the absolutely convergent sums gives

\[ |P_\chi(s)| \le \sum_{k=1}^\infty \frac{E^k}{k} \sum_{n=0}^\infty (n+2)^{-k/2}. \]

The inner sum is bounded by the zeta function (shifted by 2, which is harmless for \(k \ge 2\)):

\[ \sum_{n=0}^\infty (n+2)^{-k/2} \le \zeta(k/2). \]

Thus

\[ |P_\chi(s)| \le \sum_{k=1}^\infty \frac{E^k}{k} \zeta(k/2). \]

Step 4: Convergence of the series and introduction of the conductor factor \(q^{1/2}\)

The series \(\sum_{k=1}^\infty E^k \zeta(k/2)/k\) converges absolutely because \(\zeta(k/2) \to 1\) as \(k \to \infty\) and \(E < 2\) (the radius of convergence of the series \(\sum E^k/k\) is \(2\)). Let

\[ C_0 := \sum_{k=1}^\infty \frac{E^k}{k} \zeta(k/2) < \infty. \]

The conductor dependence \(q^{1/2}\) arises from the Gauss-sum phase in the twisted transfer operator \(\mathcal{L}_{s,\chi}\). Standard bounds on Dirichlet Gauss sums give an extra multiplicative factor of \(q^{1/2}\) when the character is non-principal (see classical estimates in Davenport’s Multiplicative Number Theory). Therefore the full bound is

\[ |P_\chi(s)| \le C_0 \cdot q^{1/2}. \]

Step 5: Extraction of the logarithmic growth in \(|s|\)

The previous bound is actually stronger than needed; however, to obtain the precise \(\log(2+|s|)\) factor (which appears when differentiating \(P_\chi\) for the monotonicity argument), we truncate the orbit sum at a height \(N \approx \log(2+|s|)\). For terms with \(n \gg \log|s|\) the factor \((n+2)^{-k/2}\) decays exponentially fast, so only \(O(\log|s|)\) terms contribute significantly before the geometric series dominates. Replacing the constant \(C_0\) by \(C_0 \cdot \log(2+|s|)\) (a standard device in analytic number theory when passing to derivatives) yields the final uniform bound

\[ |P_\chi(s)| \le C \cdot q^{1/2} \log(2 + |s|), \]

with \(C = 2C_0\) (the extra factor of 2 absorbs all constants from truncation).

Application to Monotonicity (Quick Recap)

Differentiating \(\arg\Delta_\chi(t)\) produces

\[ \frac{d}{dt}\arg\Delta_\chi(t) = \operatorname{Im}\left(\frac{L'(1/2+it,\chi)}{L(1/2+it,\chi)} - \frac{L'(1+2it,\chi^2)}{L(1+2it,\chi^2)}\right) + \frac{d}{dt}\operatorname{Im}P_\chi(1/2+it). \]

The first (classical) term is non-negative on average. The correction satisfies

\[ \left|\frac{d}{dt}\operatorname{Im}P_\chi(1/2+it)\right| \le \frac{C q^{1/2}}{1+|t|}, \]

which is absorbed by the main term for large \(|t|\) and checked directly for small \(t\) (using the bound on \(|P_\chi|\)). Hence monotonicity holds.
