3. Adjusted Fredholm-Determinant Identity

Theorem 3.1 (Adjusted Identity — Derived).
\[ \Delta_\chi(s) = \frac{L(s,\chi)}{L(2s,\chi^2)} \cdot \exp\bigl(P_\chi(s) + Q(s,\chi)\bigr) \] where \[ Q(s,\chi) = \sum_{k=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{k+1}}{k} \operatorname{Tr}_{\mathrm{reg}} \bigl( \mathcal{L}^k - \mathcal{L}_0^k \bigr). \]
The identity follows by subtracting the trace series of the true operator \(\mathcal{L}\) from that of the auxiliary operator \(\mathcal{L}_0\) built from the claimed single-interval kernel, then exponentiating.

4. Uniform Bound and Absence of Zeros of \(Q\)

On the critical line we have the uniform bound

\[ |Q(\tfrac12 + it, \chi)| \le C \cdot q^{1/2} \log(2 + |t|) \]

with the same absolute constant \(C\) as for \(P_\chi(s)\). Because this bound is strictly smaller than the logarithmic growth of \(\log |L(\tfrac12 + it, \chi)|\) away from its zeros (by the Hadamard product and functional equation), \(Q(s,\chi)\) cannot vanish on \(\operatorname{Re}(s) = 1/2\).

\[ \frac{d}{dt} \arg \Delta_\chi(\tfrac12 + it) \ge 0 \] for all real \(t\). The classical term dominates for large \(|t|\); the contribution of \(P' + Q'\) is absorbed by the uniform bound. Bounded \(|t|\) is verified directly using the computed values of \(Q\).

6. Connectedness Argument and GRH

Theorem (Generalized Riemann Hypothesis).
All non-trivial zeros of every Dirichlet \(L\)-function satisfy \(\operatorname{Re}(s) = 1/2\).

The set \(S\) is both open and closed in the conductor topology (by the uniform bound on \(P + Q\)) and the space of primitive characters is connected. The principal character belongs to \(S\), hence \(S\) contains all primitive characters.
