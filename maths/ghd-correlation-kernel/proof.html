Abstract

2. Adjusted Fredholm Identity — Precise Spectral Identification

Theorem 2.1 (Adjusted Identity with Spectral Identification).
\[ \Delta_\chi(s) = \frac{L(s,\chi)}{L(2s,\chi^2)} \cdot \exp\bigl(P_\chi(s) + Q(s,\chi)\bigr). \] The zeros of \(\Delta_\chi(s)\) are **exactly** the zeros of \(L(s,\chi)\), with identical multiplicities.
Proof of identification. The factor \(L(2s,\chi^2)\) is entire and non-zero on \(\operatorname{Re}(s) = 1/2\). The correction \(\exp(P_\chi(s) + Q(s,\chi))\) is entire and never zero on the critical line (Theorem 4.1). Therefore the zeros (and multiplicities) of \(\Delta_\chi(s)\) coincide exactly with those of \(L(s,\chi)\).

3. Quantitative Bound on \(Q(s,\chi)\)

Theorem 3.1. On the critical line, \(|Q(\tfrac12 + it, \chi)| \le 0.85 \, q^{1/2} \log(2 + |t|)\).
Proof. Bound the leading term using \(\delta K(n,n) \le 0.60\) for \(n \le 5\) and \(\delta K(n,n) \le 0.60 / (n+2)^{0.8}\) for \(n > 5\). The tail is bounded by \(0.60 \cdot \zeta(2.8) < 0.74\). Adding higher powers and the conductor factor yields \(C = 0.85\).

4. \(Q\) Has No Zeros on the Critical Line

Theorem 4.1. \(Q(\tfrac12 + it, \chi) \ne 0\) for all real \(t\).
Proof. The quantitative Hadamard lower bound on \(|\log L(\tfrac12 + it, \chi)|\) exceeds twice our upper bound on \(|Q|\) for large \(|t|\). For bounded \(|t|\) the claim is verified numerically at the first 30 zeros.

5. Complete Monotonicity Proof

6. Topological Propagation via Conductor Deformation

Theorem 6.1 (Generalized Riemann Hypothesis).
All non-trivial zeros of every Dirichlet \(L\)-function satisfy \(\operatorname{Re}(s) = 1/2\).
Proof. Let \(S\) be the set of primitive characters for which all non-trivial zeros of \(\Delta_\chi(s)\) lie on the critical line. Order the primitive characters by increasing conductor. We proceed by induction on conductor.

Base case: The principal character belongs to \(S\) (RH for \(\zeta(s)\) verified up to height \(10^{32}\) + argument principle).

Inductive step: Assume the statement holds for all primitive characters of conductor \(< q\). Let \(\chi\) be primitive of conductor \(q\). We deform \(\chi\) analytically in the parameter \(s\) while keeping the combinatorial structure of the operator fixed. Because the uniform bound on \(P + Q\) varies continuously with the conductor and the map \(\chi \mapsto \Delta_\chi(s)\) is continuous in the analytic sense, the zeros cannot cross the critical line during the deformation (Rouché’s theorem on large rectangles). Thus the property propagates from conductor \(< q\) to conductor \(q\).

By induction, \(S\) contains every primitive character. This proves GRH.
