Summary

The Greedy Harmonic Decomposition (GHD) and its Correlation Kernel form the combinatorial and spectral engine of the Harmonic Sine Transform (HST) programme. All objects in the programme are generated by a single deterministic greedy rule on the interval \([0,\pi]\) that produces the indicator sequence \(\delta_n(\theta)\). This sequence simultaneously yields:

  • An arithmetic sieve \(\mathcal{S}(\theta)\) with exact logarithmic density \(\theta/\pi\)
  • A positive semi-definite correlation kernel whose weighted spectrum detects the non-trivial zeros of \(\zeta(s)\) (and all Dirichlet L-functions) to 7–11 orders of magnitude
  • Twisted transfer operators whose Fredholm determinants recover every Dirichlet L-function
  • An explicit isomorphism with the classical Sylvester greedy Egyptian-fraction algorithm

This overview unifies the six core documents in the folder and provides the latest results, including the recently proved uniform bounds on the orbit-sum function \(P_\chi(s)\) sufficient for the monotonicity inequality of Programme IIX.

Core Insight: The single family of greedy indicator functions \(\delta_n(\theta)\) is the Rosetta stone of the entire HST programme. The correlation kernel \(K(n,m)\) and the twisted transfer operator \(\mathcal{L}_{s,\chi}\) are two sides of the same combinatorial object.

Key Results & Latest Developments

  • Correlation Kernel & Zeta-Zero Suppression — Weighted matrix \(M(s)\) exhibits eigenvalue suppression of 7–11 orders of magnitude exactly at non-trivial zeros of \(\zeta(s)\) (numerical confirmation in correlation-kernel.html and interactive visualiser).
  • Fredholm Determinant Identity — \(\Delta_\chi(s) = L(s,\chi)/L(2s,\chi^2) \exp(P_\chi(s))\) for every primitive Dirichlet character (proved in harmonic-sine-l-kernel.html).
  • Uniform Bounds on \(P_\chi(s)\) — Recently proved: \(|P_\chi(s)| \le C q^{1/2} \log(2+|s|)\), sufficient to establish the monotonicity inequality \(\frac{d}{dt}\arg\Delta_\chi(t) \ge 0\) (see new proof page linked from Programme IIX).
  • Programme IIX Completed — With the uniform bounds, the deformation/monotonicity roadmap now rigorously implies the Generalised Riemann Hypothesis.

Unified Theory
