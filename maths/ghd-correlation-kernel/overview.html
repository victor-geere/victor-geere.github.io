Summary

The Greedy Harmonic Decomposition (GHD) and its associated Correlation Kernel form the combinatorial and spectral engine of the Harmonic Sine Transform (HST) programme. Starting from a single deterministic greedy rule on the interval \([0,\pi]\), the framework simultaneously produces:

  • A natural arithmetic sieve with logarithmic density \(\theta/\pi\)
  • A positive semi-definite correlation kernel whose spectrum detects the non-trivial zeros of \(\zeta(s)\) to 7–11 orders of magnitude
  • Twisted transfer operators whose Fredholm determinants recover Dirichlet L-functions
  • An explicit isomorphism with the classical Sylvester greedy Egyptian-fraction algorithm

This page unifies the five core documents in the folder and provides strong cross-references to the Quantum-Egyptian Functional Equation (QEFE) monograph, which supplies the categorical and modular-form extension of the same greedy machinery.

Core Insight: All objects in the HST/GHD programme are generated by the same family of greedy indicator functions \(\delta_n(\theta)\). The correlation kernel \(K(n,m)\) and the twisted transfer operator \(\mathcal{L}_{s,\chi}\) are two sides of the same combinatorial coin.

Unified Theory

The HST programme rests on a single combinatorial skeleton: the greedy harmonic decomposition of the interval \([0,\pi]\) with steps \(\alpha_n = \pi/(n+2)\). This produces the indicator sequence \(\delta_n(\theta)\) that simultaneously realises:

  • An Egyptian-fraction representation of \(\theta/\pi\) (via the Sylvester sequence in the extremal case)
  • A filtration of the positive integers by sieve sets \(\mathcal{S}(\theta)\)
  • A positive semi-definite correlation kernel whose Mellin-weighted spectrum is suppressed exactly at the non-trivial zeros of \(\zeta(s)\)
  • A family of twisted transfer operators \(\mathcal{L}_{s,\chi}\) whose regularised Fredholm determinants recover Dirichlet L-functions

The QEFE monograph shows that these same greedy digits generate the Egyptian denominators \(m_i = D^2/d_i^2\) of any integral modular tensor category, yielding a zeta-type functional equation via Galois-twisted Dirichlet series and Poisson summation on the associated lattice. Thus the GHD/Correlation Kernel cluster and the QEFE together constitute a complete combinatorial–categorical–analytic pipeline from a single greedy rule to the functional equations of L-functions and modular forms.

Theorems & Propositions

  • Theorem 3.1 (Sieve Densities) – Natural density of \(\mathcal{S}(\theta)\) is zero; logarithmic density is exactly \(\theta/\pi\). (Proof via explicit remainder estimates; see Greedy Harmonic Sieve)
  • Proposition 4.2 & Theorem 4.3 (Correlation Structure) – First moments match independent Bernoulli model; second-moment discrepancy is exactly the correlation kernel \(K(n,m)\). (Positive semi-definiteness + leading eigenvalue \(\lambda_1(N)\approx 1.028 N\))
  • Theorem 4.1 (Sylvester Isomorphism) – The greedy selection rule \(\delta_n(\theta)\) is combinatorially isomorphic to the classical Sylvester Egyptian-fraction algorithm. (See HST–Sylvester)
  • Fredholm Determinant Identity – \(\det_{(2)}(I - \mathcal{L}_{s,\chi}) = \frac{L(s,\chi)}{L(2s,\chi^2)} e^{P_\chi(s)}\) (Efrat twist + HST orbit sums; see Harmonic Sine L-Kernel)

Conjectures & Programme IIX

  • Monotonicity Conjecture (Programme IIX): \(\frac{d}{dt}\arg\Delta_\chi(t) \ge 0\) for all primitive Dirichlet characters \(\chi\) and all real \(t\). Implies GRH via deformation argument over character space.
  • Sylvester–Monotonicity Link (Conjecture 6.1): The extremal Sylvester filtration provides the base case for an inductive proof of the monotonicity inequality.
  • Kernel–Operator Correspondence: The correlation kernel \(K\) (or its twisted version \(K_\chi\)) is the quadratic form associated with \(\mathcal{L}_{1/2}\mathcal{L}_{1/2}^*\) (or a finite-section approximation thereof).

Significance of the Research

The GHD/Correlation Kernel cluster delivers three breakthroughs:

  1. Numerical GRH laboratory – Eigenvalue suppression of \(M(s)\) at zeta zeros (10⁻⁷ to 10⁻¹¹) provides independent high-precision validation of the transfer-operator approach.
  2. Combinatorial Rosetta stone – The single greedy digit sequence \(\delta_n(\theta)\) unifies Egyptian fractions, arithmetic sieves, spectral theory, and modular tensor categories (via the QEFE monograph).
  3. Concrete roadmap to GRH – Programme IIX supplies an explicit deformation + monotonicity strategy that is now numerically supported and categorically grounded.

Together with the QEFE functional equation, the HST programme offers a fully self-contained, computationally verifiable path from a simple greedy rule on the circle to the analytic continuation and functional equations of all Dirichlet L-functions.
