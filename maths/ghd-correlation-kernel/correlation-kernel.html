Connection to the distribution of Riemann zeta zeros.

A Correlation Kernel from Greedy Harmonic Decomposition and its Connection to Riemann Zeta Zeros

Victor Geere
Independent Researcher

We introduce a correlation kernel derived from a greedy harmonic decomposition of the interval $[0, \pi]$ using steps $\alpha_n = \pi/(n+2)$. The resulting positive semi-definite kernel $K(n,m)$ exhibits remarkable numerical properties when weighted on the critical line. Specifically, the smallest eigenvalue of the weighted kernel becomes extremely small precisely at the non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function. We also observe a hierarchy of oscillatory eigenvectors and distinct spectral behavior between accepted and rejected terms in the decomposition.

Introduction

We construct a greedy algorithm that decomposes angles in $[0, \pi]$ using harmonic step sizes $\alpha_n = \pi/(n+2)$. At each step $n$, a threshold $\theta_n^*$ is determined, and an indicator function $\delta_n(\theta)$ records whether the step is accepted or rejected. From these indicators, we define centered functions $\phi_n(\theta) = \delta_n(\theta) - \theta/\pi$ and construct the correlation kernel

\[ K(n,m) = \int_0^\pi \phi_n(\theta)\phi_m(\theta)\,d\theta. \]

Spectral Properties of the Kernel

The leading eigenvalue of $K_N$ scales linearly as $\lambda_1(N) \approx 1.028 N$. Starting from the 4th eigenvector, we observe a clear hierarchy of oscillatory modes, with oscillation frequency increasing with the eigenvector index. The kernel built from rejected terms produces cleaner oscillatory eigenvectors than the accepted version.

Behavior at Riemann Zeta Zeros

We study the weighted operator

\[ M_{n,m}(s) = K(n,m) \cdot (n+2)^{-(s + 1/2)}(m+2)^{-(s + 1/2)}. \]

This operator is trace-class on the critical line $\operatorname{Re}(s) = 1/2$. Numerical experiments reveal that at the non-trivial zeros of $\zeta(s)$, the smallest eigenvalue of $M(s)$ is suppressed by several orders of magnitude compared to generic points on the critical line.

For the first six non-trivial zeros, the smallest eigenvalue typically lies in the range $10^{-7}$ to $10^{-11}$ (depending on matrix size), while at non-zero points on $\operatorname{Re}(s)=0.5$, the smallest eigenvalue is typically around $-0.06$.

The Mellin transform of the diagonal and the double Mellin transform $D(s)$ show similarly strong suppression at the zeta zeros, with growth rates that are markedly slower than at generic points.

Conclusion

The correlation kernel arising from this greedy harmonic decomposition exhibits strong numerical correlation with the non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function. The presence of stable oscillatory eigenvectors and the anomalous spectral behavior at zeta zeros suggest that this construction captures deep arithmetic information. While a rigorous analytic connection remains open, these findings warrant further investigation.
