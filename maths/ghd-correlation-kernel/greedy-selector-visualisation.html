1. Definition (Exact from GHD Papers)

\[ \delta_n(\theta) = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if remainder } \theta_n \ge \alpha_n = \frac{\pi}{n+2}, \\ 0 & \text{otherwise}, \end{cases} \qquad \theta_{n+1} = \theta_n - \delta_n(\theta)\,\alpha_n \]

4. New Graph: How the Greedy Indicator Selects Zeta Zeros

Description of visualisation: This graph shows the smallest eigenvalue \(\lambda_{\min}\) of the weighted correlation-kernel matrix \(M_{n,m}(s)\) (N=40) built from the greedy indicators \(\delta_n(\theta)\). The eigenvalue is dramatically suppressed (by 7–11 orders of magnitude) only when \(s\) is exactly at a non-trivial zero of \(\zeta(s)\). Use the controls below to perturb the point \(s = 0.5 + \operatorname{Re}(s) + i(t_k + \operatorname{Im}(s))\) and see that the dip is deepest precisely when both offsets are zero.
