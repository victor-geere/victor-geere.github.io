1. Introduction and Remainder Map

Description: The greedy algorithm defines a scaled remainder process whose continuous limit is the classical Berry–Keating sawtooth flow.

\[ x_{n+1} = \left(1 + \frac{1}{n+2}\right)\bigl(x_n - \mathbf{1}_{x_n \ge 1}\bigr), \] \[ x(\tau) = \bigl\{ x_0 \, e^\tau \bigr\}, \quad \tau = \log(n+2). \]
Proof Sketch: Define the scaled remainder \(x_n = (n+2)r_n/\pi\). Split on whether the next digit is taken (\(x_n \ge 1\)) or not. The free branch multiplies by \((n+3)/(n+2)\). Telescoping the product yields the exponential flow; integer folding produces the fractional-part map.

2. Transfer Operator and Fredholm Determinant

Description: The transfer operator \(\mathcal{L}_s\) satisfies an exact determinant identity with \(\zeta(s)\).

\[ \det(I - \mathcal{L}_s) = \frac{\zeta(s)}{\zeta(2s)}\,e^{P(s)}, \] where \(P(s)\) is entire and zero-free on \(\mathbb{C}\).
Proof Sketch: Nuclearity of \(\mathcal{L}_s\) implies the Fredholm determinant is entire. The principal product over first-hit cylinders reproduces the Euler product of \(\zeta(s)\). The quadratic double-angle relation supplies \(\zeta(2s)\). The residual product over deeper orbits converges absolutely and is zero-free.

3. Self-Adjoint Operator H

Description: The sawtooth flow quantises to a self-adjoint Hilbert–Pólya operator H.

\[ H = -i\left(x \frac{\partial}{\partial x} + \tfrac12\right), \quad D(H) = \{\phi\in H^1([0,1]) : \phi(1^-)=\phi(0^+)\}. \]
Proof Sketch: Koopman generator of \(U(\tau)f(x)=f(\{x e^\tau\})\) is \(x\partial_x\) with transmission jump. Weyl symmetrisation yields H. Integration by parts shows symmetry on D(H). Stone’s theorem applied to the unitary group proves essential self-adjointness.

6. Trace Formula in the Haar Basis

Description: The trace of the unitary group generated by H is an explicit sum over roots of the local quadratics.

\[ \operatorname{tr}(e^{-i\tau H}) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \bigl(e^{-i\tau\lambda_{n,+}} + e^{-i\tau\lambda_{n,-}}\bigr). \]
Proof Sketch: Haar basis diagonalises H into independent 2×2 blocks. Trace of each block is the sum of exponentials of its eigenvalues. Orthogonality across scales gives the full sum.
