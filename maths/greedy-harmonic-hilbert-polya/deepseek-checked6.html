10. Greedy Condition on the Dirichlet Eta Function (unproven equivalence)

⚠️ Honest status: The document “Rigorous Equivalence Between the Riemann Hypothesis and a Greedy Condition on the Dirichlet Eta Function” claims Theorem 4.1 (RH ⇔ GC). The forward direction (RH ⇒ GC) is not proved; it relies on an unsubstantiated claim about the Riemann–Siegel formula. The reverse direction (GC ⇒ RH) is sketched but lacks rigorous error control. The equivalence remains unproven. The definitions and standard lemmas are included for completeness.

10.1 Notation and the Greedy Condition

\[ \eta(s)=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^{n-1}}{n^{s}},\qquad S_N(s)=\sum_{n=1}^{N}\frac{(-1)^{n-1}}{n^{s}},\qquad w_N(s)=N^{-s}. \]
Definition · Greedy Condition (GC)
A non‑trivial zero \(s\) of \(\eta(s)\) satisfies GC iff \[ \boxed{\;\operatorname{Re}\!\bigl(\overline{S_{N-1}(s)}\,w_N(s)\bigr)\le 0\qquad\forall\,N\ge 1\;}. \]

■ Well‑defined. The condition involves only finite sums; no convergence issues.

10.2 Standard lemmas (proved in the literature)

Lemma 10.1 · Approximate functional equation for \(\eta(s)\)
For fixed \(s\) with \(0<\operatorname{Re}s<1\) and any \(N\ge 1\), \[ \eta(s)=S_N(s)+(-1)^N\frac{N^{1-s}}{1-s}+R_N(s),\qquad R_N(s)=O(N^{-\sigma-1})\;(N\to\infty). \] If \(\eta(s)=0\) then \[ S_N(s)=-(-1)^N\frac{N^{1-s}}{1-s}+R_N(s). \]

■ Proved. Standard Euler–Maclaurin summation; see Titchmarsh, Theorem 3.1.

Lemma 10.2 · Riemann–Siegel formula for \(\eta(s)\) (classical part)
Let \(s=\tfrac12+it\), \(t>0\), and \(M=\lfloor\sqrt{t/(2\pi)}\rfloor\). Then \[ \eta(s)=\sum_{n=1}^{M}\frac{(-1)^{n-1}}{n^{s}} +e^{-i\theta(t)}\sum_{n=1}^{M}\frac{(-1)^{n-1}}{n^{1-s}} +O(t^{-1/4}), \] with \(\theta(t)=\arg\Gamma\!\bigl(\tfrac14+\tfrac{it}{2}\bigr)-\tfrac{t}{2}\log\pi\).

■ Proved. Classical Riemann–Siegel formula adapted to the alternating series.

⚠ Unproven addition: The document further asserts that “for zeros on the critical line, the partial sums satisfy GC for all \(N\).” This claim is not established by the Riemann–Siegel formula alone and is not proved in the document. It is cited to an unpublished reference [2].

10.3 Claimed equivalence (Theorem 4.1 of the document)

Claim · Theorem 4.1 (unproven)
The Riemann Hypothesis is equivalent to the statement that every non‑trivial zero of \(\eta(s)\) satisfies the Greedy Condition.

⚠ Unproven. Both directions of the claimed proof contain gaps.

10.4 Sketch of the reverse direction (GC ⇒ RH)

The following is the asymptotic sketch given in the document. It is not a rigorous proof; error‑term control and uniformity are not established.

Assume every zero satisfies GC. Let \(s\) be a zero with \(0<\sigma<1\).

Case \(\sigma<1/2\). From Lemma 10.1, \(S_{N-1}(s)=-(-1)^{N-1}\frac{(N-1)^{1-s}}{1-s}+O(N^{-\sigma-1})\). Expanding \((N-1)^{1-\bar{s}}N^{-s}\) for large \(N\) yields

\[ \operatorname{Re}\!\bigl(\overline{S_{N-1}(s)}\,N^{-s}\bigr) =-(-1)^{N-1}\frac{N^{1-2\sigma}}{|1-s|^2} \bigl[(1-\sigma)\cos\tfrac{t}{N}+t\sin\tfrac{t}{N}\bigr] +O(N^{-2\sigma-1}). \]

For large \(N\), the bracket is positive. The leading term behaves like \(-(-1)^{N-1}C N^{1-2\sigma}\) with \(C>0\). Since \(\sigma<1/2\), \(1-2\sigma>0\) and the term grows. For even \(N\) (\((-1)^{N-1}=-1\)), the expression is asymptotically \(+C N^{1-2\sigma}>0\), contradicting GC. Hence no zero can have \(\sigma<1/2\).

Case \(\sigma>1/2\). The functional equation for \(\eta(s)\) implies that \(1-s\) is also a zero, and \(\operatorname{Re}(1-s)=1-\sigma<1/2\). By the previous case, \(1-s\) would violate GC. Hence no zero can have \(\sigma>1/2\).

Therefore \(\sigma=1/2\) for all zeros.

⚠ Gaps: (i) The error term \(O(N^{-2\sigma-1})\) is not shown to be smaller than the main term uniformly in \(s\); (ii) no bound on “sufficiently large \(N\)” is provided; (iii) the functional equation for \(\eta(s)\) is stated without verification.

10.5 Forward direction (RH ⇒ GC) — not proved

The document asserts that if all zeros lie on the critical line, then GC holds. The argument relies on the unproven part of Lemma 10.2 and is not established.
