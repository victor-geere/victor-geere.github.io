🔴 Honest status — Please read first

This document contains every rigorously derived formula in the GHF programme. It does not contain a proof of the Riemann Hypothesis or the Generalised Riemann Hypothesis.

What is proved: The greedy harmonic decomposition converges at rate O(1/N). The correlation kernel K(n,m) has a closed rational form. The Gram matrix of centred indicators has a diagonal‑plus‑rank‑2 structure whose Fredholm determinant factorises exactly. The greedy Dirichlet sub‑sum E(θ,s) admits meromorphic continuation to ℂ with a single pole at s = 1. The transfer operator ℒs on H²(𝔻) is trace‑class for Re(s) > 1/2 and is similar to the Mayer operator. The eml operator eml(x,y) = exp(x) − ln(y) is universal and can express the harmonic‑sine fusion step. The geometric reformulation via twin conical helices and the Riemann helix is a consistent geometric translation of the Weil explicit formula.

What is NOT proved: The Fredholm determinant identity det(I − ℒs) = ζ(s)/ζ(2s)·eP(s). Self‑adjointness of ℒ1/2+it. Bijectivity between zeta zeros and zeros of the mismatch functional. Any version of RH or GRH.

The exhaustion gap: The framework proves that every non‑trivial zero of ζ(s) corresponds to a zero of the mismatch functional Δ(t). The converse — that every real zero of Δ(t) corresponds to a zeta zero — is logically equivalent to RH and has not been proved.

1. Greedy Harmonic Decomposition

5. Explicit Fredholm Determinant of the Hybrid Operator \(M_\chi(s)\)

ℹ️ Honest status: The determinant formula for the hybrid operator \(M_\chi(s)\) is a rigorous algebraic consequence of the Weinstein–Aronszajn lemma applied to the diagonal‑plus‑rank‑2 structure (Theorem 4.2). However, the connection of this determinant to \(\zeta(s)/\zeta(2s)\) is not proved in this document. The derivation below stops at the level of the infinite product and the 2×2 correction; evaluating those in closed form in terms of the Riemann zeta function remains an open problem.
Definition · Hybrid operator \(M_\chi(s)\)
\[ M_\chi(s) = \Lambda(s) + X(s)\, B\, X(s)^T, \] where \[ \Lambda(s) = \operatorname{diag}\!\bigl( \chi(n+2)\,(n+2)^{-s} \bigr)_{n=0}^\infty, \] \(X(s)\) is the \(N\times 2\) matrix whose columns are the two leading eigenvectors of the correlation kernel \(K\) (weighted by \((n+2)^{-s/2}\)), and \(B\) is the fixed \(2\times 2\) matrix determined by the threshold identity \(\theta_n^* = \pi/(n+2)\) and the leading spectral data of \(K\).
Theorem 5.1 · Explicit Fredholm Determinant of \(M_\chi(s)\)
\[ \boxed{ \det(I - M_\chi(s)) = \Bigl( \prod_{n=0}^\infty \bigl(1 - \chi(n+2)(n+2)^{-s}\bigr) \Bigr) \cdot \det\!\bigl(I_2 - B\, G(s)\bigr) }, \] with \[ G(s) = X(s)^T \bigl(I - \Lambda(s)\bigr)^{-1} X(s). \]

■ Proved (algebraically). Follows from the Weinstein–Aronszajn matrix‑determinant lemma for a diagonal‑plus‑rank‑2 perturbation, assuming the necessary trace‑class conditions hold (which they do for \(\operatorname{Re}s>1/2\) after appropriate regularisation of the infinite matrices). The infinite product is regularised via the greedy Dirichlet sub‑sum (Section 7).

5.1 Alternative closed‑form correlation kernel

The corrected kernel (used in the above construction) can also be written as

\[ K(n,n) = \frac{\pi}{3} - \frac{\pi}{n+2} + \frac{\pi}{(n+2)^2}, \qquad K(n,m) = \frac{\pi}{3} - \frac{\pi}{\max(n+2,m+2)} + \frac{\pi}{2(n+2)^2} + \frac{\pi}{2(m+2)^2}\;(n\neq m). \]

These expressions are algebraically equivalent to those in Section 3.1 and yield identical numerical values.

5.2 The missing link to \(\zeta(s)\)

To obtain the claimed identity \(\det(I-\mathcal{L}_s)=\zeta(s)/\zeta(2s)\cdot e^{P(s)}\), one would need to:

  1. Show that the regularised product \(\prod(1-\chi(n+2)(n+2)^{-s})\) equals \(\zeta(s)/\zeta(2s)\) up to an entire factor with no zeros in \(\operatorname{Re}s>1/2\).
  2. Evaluate the 2×2 determinant \(\det(I_2-B\,G(s))\) and absorb it into the entire function \(P(s)\).
  3. Prove that the resulting entire function does not introduce extraneous zeros.

None of these steps are carried out in the document under review. The “Five‑Step Proof” mentioned there is a sketch; the required trace‑norm closeness bounds, Rouché arguments, and explicit evaluations are not provided.

5.3 Numerical illustration

The document includes an interactive JavaScript demo that builds the correlation kernel and extracts its two leading eigenvectors. This confirms the rank‑2 structure numerically but does not constitute a proof of any zeta‑regularised identity.
