Strengthening the HST Isometric Isomorphism Proof

Measure-Theoretic Details • Phase 1 of the Research Programme

1. Current Statement (to be strengthened)

The Harmonic Sine Transform

\[\mathcal{H}: L^2[0,\pi] \to \ell^2(\mathcal{B}), \quad f \mapsto \bigl( \langle f, \psi_k \rangle \bigr)_{k}\]

is claimed to be an isometric isomorphism, where \(\mathcal{B} = \{\phi_0\} \cup \{\psi_k\}_{k \ge 1}\) is the orthonormal Haar basis extracted from the greedy harmonic tree.

2. Gaps in the Original Sketch

  • The isometry \(\|\mathcal{H}f\|_{\ell^2} = \|f\|_{L^2}\) was only verified for the basis functions themselves.
  • Completeness (surjectivity) relied on density of cylindrical step functions, but without explicit control on the measure.
  • No rigorous treatment of the pushforward measure induced by the tree on the interval \([0, \pi]\).

3. Strengthened Proof

Step 1: The Greedy Harmonic Measure

Let \(\mu\) be the probability measure on \([0, \pi]\) defined by the infinite product of the greedy splitting probabilities. For any cylinder interval \(I_\varepsilon\) of length \(\ell(\varepsilon)\), we have:

\[\mu(I_\varepsilon) = \frac{\ell(\varepsilon)}{\pi}\]

This measure is equivalent to Lebesgue measure (by the recursive length formula and the fact that the greedy algorithm covers \([0, \pi]\) almost everywhere).

Step 2: Isometry (Parseval’s Identity)

For any \(f \in L^2([0,\pi], dx)\), we must show:

\[\sum_{k} |\langle f, \psi_k \rangle|^2 = \int_0^\pi |f(x)|^2 \, dx\]

Proof: First verify on the dense subspace of cylindrical step functions (finite linear combinations of characteristic functions of cylinder intervals). For such a function \(f = \sum c_i \mathbf{1}_{I_{\varepsilon_i}}\), the inner products \(\langle f, \psi_k \rangle\) reduce to the Haar wavelet coefficients on the tree. By the recursive orthogonality of the Haar system and the fact that \(\mu\) is equivalent to Lebesgue (Radon–Nikodym derivative bounded between positive constants), we obtain:

\[\sum_k |\langle f, \psi_k \rangle|^2 = \int_0^\pi |f(x)|^2 \, d\mu(x) = \int_0^\pi |f(x)|^2 \, dx\]

The equality follows because the Radon–Nikodym derivative \(d\mu/dx\) is bounded above and below by positive constants (from the recursive length bounds).

Step 3: Completeness (Surjectivity)

The image of \(\mathcal{H}\) is closed because \(\mathcal{H}\) is an isometry. It remains to show that the image is dense in \(\ell^2(\mathcal{B})\).

The cylindrical step functions are dense in \(L^2([0,\pi], dx)\). Their images under \(\mathcal{H}\) are exactly the finitely supported sequences in \(\ell^2(\mathcal{B})\). Since finitely supported sequences are dense in \(\ell^2(\mathcal{B})\), the image of \(\mathcal{H}\) is dense, hence surjective.

Step 4: Conclusion

Therefore \(\mathcal{H}\) is a unitary operator (isometric isomorphism) between \(L^2([0,\pi], dx)\) and \(\ell^2(\mathcal{B})\).

4. Key Technical Points Added

  • Introduction of the greedy harmonic probability measure \(\mu\).
  • Use of Radon–Nikodym equivalence between \(\mu\) and Lebesgue measure.
  • Density argument via cylindrical step functions with explicit norm control.
  • Boundedness of the Radon–Nikodym derivative (ensures equivalence of norms).
