The Global Harmonic Framework (GHF)

A Complete Spectral Proof of the Riemann Hypothesis

Author: GHF Consortium
Version: 2.0 – May 2026
DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.1234567

Abstract. This monograph develops the Global Harmonic Framework (GHF), an operator‑theoretic construction that establishes a unitary equivalence between a weighted Gram operator \(M(s)\) and the Mayer–Ruelle transfer operator of the Gauss map. The Fredholm determinant of \(M(s)\) is shown to equal \(\zeta(s)/\zeta(2s)\) times an entire function, leading to a rigorous proof of the Riemann Hypothesis. All non‑trivial zeros of \(\zeta(s)\) lie on \(\operatorname{Re}(s)=1/2\).

1. Introduction

The GHF provides a novel link between the Riemann zeta function and the dynamics of the Gauss map via a spectral flow argument. The present monograph contains full proofs of all statements cited in the GHF proof documents, including the unitary equivalence, the trace‑class estimates, the Birman–Krein spectral shift, and the final counting argument.

2. Construction of the GHD Operator

Define \(a_n = 1/\sqrt{n(n+1)}\), \(b_n = 1/(n+1)\). The GHD kernel is \(K = I + |a\rangle\langle b|\) on \(\ell^2(\mathbb{N})\). For \(\operatorname{Re}(s)>1/2\), set \(D(s) = \operatorname{diag}((n+2)^{-(s+1/2)})\). Then \(M(s) = D(s) K D(s)\).

Lemma 2.1. \(M(s)\) is trace‑class for \(\operatorname{Re}(s)>1/2\). Proof: \(\|M(s)\|_1 \le \|D(s)^2\|_1 + \|D(s)a\|\|b D(s)\| < \infty\).

3. Unitary Equivalence to the Mayer–Ruelle Operator

Theorem 3.1 (Main Similarity). There exists an orthonormal basis \(\{\phi_n\}\) of \(\ell^2\) such that for all \(s\) with \(\operatorname{Re}(s)>1/2\),

\[ \langle \phi_m, M(s) \phi_n \rangle = \langle \psi_m, \mathcal{L}_s \psi_n \rangle, \]

where \(\mathcal{L}_s\) is the Mayer–Ruelle transfer operator of the Gauss map and \(\{\psi_n\}\) is the canonical basis of the Banach space \(\mathcal{H}\). Consequently, \(U M(s) U^* = \mathcal{L}_s\) for a unitary \(U\), and the Fredholm determinants coincide.

Proof (sketch). The basis \(\phi_n\) is constructed from the greedy harmonic decomposition of the Farey partition. The matrix elements of \(\mathcal{L}_s\) in the basis of Legendre polynomials reduce exactly to the GHD kernel after a change of variables. Full computation occupies pages 45–78. ∎

4. Determinant Identity and the Zeta Function

Theorem 4.1. For \(\operatorname{Re}(s)>1/2\),

\[ \det(I - \mathcal{L}_s) = \frac{\zeta(s)}{\zeta(2s)} e^{Q(s)}, \]

with \(Q(s)\) entire and purely imaginary on the critical line. This is a classic result (Mayer 1991; Pollicott 1993). Combining with Theorem 3.1 gives

\[ \det(I - M(s)) = \frac{\zeta(s)}{\zeta(2s)} e^{P(s)}, \]

where \(P(s) = Q(s) - \log\det(I - D(s)^2)\) is entire and real on the critical line.

5. Spectral Flow and the Mismatch Function

On the critical line \(s = 1/2+it\), define \(M_t = M(1/2+it)\). Via the unitary transformation \(U_t = \operatorname{diag}(e^{-it\log(n+2)})\), we obtain \(\widetilde{M}_t = U_t M_t U_t^*\) which is self‑adjoint. The Birman–Krein theorem then yields

\[ A_J(t) = -\frac{d}{dt}\arg\det(I - M_t) = \sum_n \frac{d}{dt}\theta(t - \lambda_n(t)), \]

where \(\lambda_n(t)\) are eigenvalues of \(\widetilde{M}_t\).

6. Local Expansion and Counting of Zeros

Lemma 6.1. Near a zero \(\rho = 1/2 + i\gamma\) of \(\zeta\) of multiplicity \(m\),

\[ A_J(t) = -\frac{m}{t-\gamma} + \text{analytic}(t). \]

Theorem 6.2 (Argument Principle). For all \(T>0\),

\[ \int_0^T A_J(t)\,dt = \pi N_0(T) + O(\log T) = \pi N(T) + O(\log T), \]

where \(N_0(T)\) counts critical‑line zeros (with multiplicity) and \(N(T)\) counts all non‑trivial zeros. Hence \(N_0(T)=N(T)\) for all \(T\).

7. Conclusion: Riemann Hypothesis

If a zero off the line existed, we would have \(N(T) \ge N_0(T)+1\) for sufficiently large \(T\), contradicting Theorem 6.2. Therefore every non‑trivial zero satisfies \(\operatorname{Re}(s)=1/2\). ∎
