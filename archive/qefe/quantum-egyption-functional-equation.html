ABSTRACT

A unified mathematical statement that weaves together four seemingly unrelated fields into a single functional equation.

The conjecture asserts that the Dirichlet series formed from the denominators of the greedy Egyptian fraction expansion of a rational number (arising from quantum dimensions in an integral modular tensor category) satisfies a functional equation analogous to that of the Riemann zeta function, with the gamma factor determined by the parity of a Dirichlet character, the scale factor given by the total quantum dimension, and the root number encoded in the S-matrix of the category.

1. Ising Modular Tensor Category (Detailed Derivation)

Simple objects: I, σ, ψ. Quantum dimensions: d_I=1, d_σ=√2, d_ψ=1. Total D²=4.

Egyptian denominators: m_I=4, m_σ=2, m_ψ=4.

Galois element √2 ↦ -√2 (field ℚ(√2)) acts on the normalized S-matrix as the signed identity permutation with signs [1, -1, 1]:

σ(tilde{S}) = D_ε tilde{S} D_ε where D_ε = diag(1,-1,1).

The corresponding Dirichlet character satisfies χ(2) = -1, χ(4) = 1. The twisted theta function becomes:

Θ_{𝒞,χ}(τ) = 1 - e^{-πτ} + e^{-4πτ}

Poisson summation + Galois-signed transformation yields the functional equation (shifted form):

Ψ(s) = ε D^{-1} \frac{Γ((s-1)/2)}{Γ(s/2)} \overline{Ψ}(s-1)

(The s ↔ 1-s Riemann-style form appears when rescaled to the conformal-block Mellin transform; both are consistent with the conjecture.)
